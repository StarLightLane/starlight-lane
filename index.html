Questions, answered

The fine print, unfined.

How is your pricing different from the processor I have now? +
Most processors use tiered or bundled pricing, which sounds simple but hides where your money actually goes. We use interchange-plus: you pay the real card network cost (interchange) plus a small, transparent markup. You see every line. No "qualified" vs "non-qualified" games. No surprise junk fees.
What exactly is a holdback and why would one exist? +
A holdback (also called a reserve) is money your processor keeps from your deposits as insurance against chargebacks. They're often justified — but they're also often forgotten about, overstated, or held long after they should've been released. We audit those accounts, push for release, and recover what's yours. We only get paid when you do.
What does "AI integration" actually do for my business? +
Three practical things. It flags fraudulent transactions before they settle. It watches for patterns that predict disputes — so you can issue refunds before a cardholder calls their bank. And it matches deposits to invoices automatically, so your bookkeeper doesn't spend half their month reconciling.
Do I need to replace my current hardware? +
Usually, no. We work with most major terminals and gateways already on the market. If you do need new hardware, we ship it free and walk you through setup. Most switchovers take less than a business day with zero interruption.
